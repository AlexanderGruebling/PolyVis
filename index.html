About PolyVis

Polysomnography visualization tool for sleep apnea diagnosis

Purpose

PolyVis is a clinical visualization tool designed to help sleep physicians and researchers explore polysomnography (PSG) study data for the diagnosis of sleep apnea. It provides an interactive, browser-based interface for reviewing overnight sleep recordings without requiring proprietary software.

The tool displays key physiological signals collected during a sleep study — including electroencephalography (EEG), oxygen saturation (SaO₂), electrocardiography (ECG), and respiratory effort (thoracic and abdominal belts) — alongside hypnogram, annotated arousal and respiratory events, and computed clinical metrics.

Clinical Metrics

PolyVis computes standard sleep apnea indices directly in the browser using DuckDB WASM:

  • AHI (Apnea-Hypopnea Index) — average events per hour of sleep
  • ODI (Oxygen Desaturation Index) — desaturation events per hour
  • SaO₂ statistics — minimum, mean, and time spent below 88% and 90%
  • Total sleep time and event counts

Technical Stack

Built with vgplot / mosaic for declarative, interactive visualization grammar, DuckDB WASM for in-browser analytical queries, and vanilla JavaScript with a dark-themed card-based layout.

Data Source

The data comes from the Sleep Heart Health Study (SHHS), a multi-center cohort study conducted by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI) in collaboration with the University of California. The study collected overnight polysomnography recordings from thousands of participants to investigate the cardiovascular consequences of sleep-disordered breathing.

Each recording in PolyVis comprises:

  • Signals (EEG, SaO₂, ECG, THOR RES, ABDO RES)
  • Hypnogram (sleep stage annotations every 30 seconds)
  • Arousal events
  • Respiratory events
